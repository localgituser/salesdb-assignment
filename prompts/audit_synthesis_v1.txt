# Audit Synthesis Prompt v1 — Top 5 Gap Narratives
# Model: claude-sonnet-4-6
# Phase: phase_2
# Inputs: haiku_rankings_json, top5_sector_detail_json, size_quality_json
# Output: top 5 gap narratives covering industry + size dimensions

You are writing the definitive gap findings for a B2B Sales Intelligence platform's US market
coverage audit. A Haiku agent has ranked actionable NAICS sectors by commercial relevance and
enrichment priority, and identified the key size-dimension gap. Your job is to synthesise the
top 5 structural gaps into crisp, credible findings that a sales team or engineering pod could
act on.

The gaps must cover more than one dimension. The brief asks for gaps by industry, sub-region
(state), company size, and any other load-bearing dimension. At minimum, your top 5 should
include at least one size-dimension gap and at least one cross-dimension finding if the data
supports it.

## Output Format

Return a JSON object:

{
  "synthesis_model": "claude-sonnet-4-6",
  "prompt_version": "audit_synthesis_v1",
  "top_5_gaps": [
    {
      "rank": 1,
      "gap_id": "short_slug_e.g._retail_sourcing",
      "dimension": "industry | size | industry×size | industry×state",
      "naics_code": "XX or null if size-only gap",
      "naics_desc": "... or null",
      "headline": "one sentence: what is missing and why it matters",
      "prevalence": "concrete figure: X% coverage / N states / M records affected",
      "root_cause": "1-2 sentences on why this gap exists",
      "commercial_impact": "1-2 sentences: which buyer, which deal motion, which churn signal",
      "enrichment_approach": "1 sentence: source type and method to close it",
      "confidence": 0.0,
      "confidence_rationale": "one sentence on what limits confidence"
    }
  ],
  "cross_gap_pattern": "1-2 sentences on the common thread across the top 5",
  "recommended_phase4_target": "gap_id and one-sentence rationale for why this is the best PoC target"
}

Rules:
- headline must be self-contained — assume the reader has not seen the underlying data.
- commercial_impact must name a real buyer type (e.g. "outbound AEs targeting retail SMBs").
- Do NOT invent figures — cite only values present in the input data.
- Include at least one gap where dimension = "size" or "industry×size".
- confidence is your calibrated estimate that the gap is real and actionable (0.0–1.0).
- Return ONLY the JSON object. No markdown, no preamble.
