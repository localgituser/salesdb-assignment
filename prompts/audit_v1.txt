# Audit Prompt v1 — Sector Ranking & Root Cause
# Model: claude-haiku-4-5-20251001
# Phase: phase_2
# Input: sector_summary_json (19 NAICS sectors with coverage metrics)
# Output: ranked sectors with root_cause, gap_type, commercial_relevance, confidence

You are auditing a US company dataset (~4.16M records) for a B2B Sales Intelligence platform.
The dataset is sourced from LinkedIn-style company profiles aggregated across public web sources.
Your comparator is SUSB 2022 (employer firms) + NES 2023 (non-employer sole proprietors), which
represents the US Census Bureau's count of all legal business entities.

Your task: assess each NAICS sector's coverage gap and classify it.

## Context

Coverage ratio = our_records / comparator_total. A ratio of 0.10 means we have 10% of what
the Census says exists. This is NOT necessarily a problem — it depends on the gap type.

## Gap Types

- SOURCING_LIMIT: The missing businesses are structurally absent from LinkedIn-style data
  (e.g., NES non-employers: sole-proprietor truckers, freelance cleaners, individual agents).
  These cannot be closed by enriching existing records. Flag as low enrichment priority.

- ENTITY_MISMATCH: Census counts legal entities (each LLC separately); our data may merge
  parent+subsidiary or exclude shell companies. Not a real coverage gap.

- LABELING_GAP: Companies in this sector exist in our data but are mislabeled under a
  different industry code. Enrichment (industry reclassification) would close this.

- SOURCING_GAP: Real businesses that belong on the platform are absent. Can be closed by
  targeted acquisition from specialized directories, registries, or web crawls.

- MIXED: Combination of the above — specify the dominant type.

## Commercial Relevance for Sales Intelligence

Score 1-5 (5 = highest) based on:
- Is this sector an active B2B buyer? (do they buy SaaS, services, or hire vendors?)
- Would a sales team pay for better coverage here?
- Is the gap in employer firms (>1 employee) or dominated by NES sole proprietors?

## Input

The following JSON contains 19 NAICS sectors with aggregated coverage statistics across
all US states in scope (48 Tier A + Tier B states):

{{SECTOR_SUMMARY_JSON}}

## Output Format

Return a JSON object with this exact structure:

{
  "audit_model": "claude-haiku-4-5-20251001",
  "prompt_version": "audit_v1",
  "ranked_sectors": [
    {
      "rank": 1,
      "naics_code": "XX",
      "naics_desc": "...",
      "overall_coverage_pct": 0.0,
      "high_gap_states": 0,
      "gap_type": "SOURCING_LIMIT | ENTITY_MISMATCH | LABELING_GAP | SOURCING_GAP | MIXED",
      "gap_type_reasoning": "one sentence",
      "commercial_relevance": 1,
      "commercial_relevance_reasoning": "one sentence",
      "enrichment_priority": "HIGH | MEDIUM | LOW | SKIP",
      "confidence": 0.0,
      "key_caveat": "one sentence or null"
    }
  ],
  "top_5_for_synthesis": ["naics_code_1", "naics_code_2", "naics_code_3", "naics_code_4", "naics_code_5"],
  "audit_notes": "2-3 sentences on any cross-sector patterns or methodological flags"
}

Rules:
- Rank by enrichment_priority DESC, then commercial_relevance DESC, then coverage_pct ASC.
- SKIP sectors that are SOURCING_LIMIT with commercial_relevance <= 2 (no action possible).
- top_5_for_synthesis must be the top 5 enrichment-actionable sectors (no SKIP sectors).
- confidence reflects your certainty in the gap_type classification (0.0–1.0).
- Return ONLY the JSON object. No markdown, no preamble.
